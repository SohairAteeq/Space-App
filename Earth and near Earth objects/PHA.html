(162173) Ryugu

  • Size: Roughly 1 kilometer in diameter.
  • Composition: It is a carbon-rich asteroid, studied closely by Japan's Hayabusa2 mission.
  • Surface: Ryugu has a rugged surface covered in large boulders and a low density.
  • Orbit: It has a near-Earth orbit but does not pose an immediate threat.
  • Potentially Hazardous: Yes, due to its size and proximity.
  • Effect on Earth:
    • While it does not pose a direct threat, an impact would cause serious local damage and potential climate disruption due to its size and energy release.

(3200) Phaethon

  • Size: Approximately 5.1 kilometers in diameter.
  • Comet-like Behavior: Although it's classified as an asteroid, Phaethon displays comet-like activity when it approaches the Sun, releasing dust.
  • Orbit: It has an eccentric orbit that brings it close to the Sun, causing it to heat up and shed material.
  • Potentially Hazardous: Yes, classified as a PHA.
  • Effect on Earth:
    • Responsible for the Geminid meteor shower, though the material shed is not dangerous.
    • A collision with Earth would result in a significant global impact, causing widespread damage.

(52768) 1998 OR2

  • Size: Approximately 2 kilometers in diameter.
  • Close Approaches: It makes periodic close approaches to Earth, with one significant pass in 2020.
  • Orbit: It has a long orbital period of around 3.68 years and comes close to Earth, though not immediately threatening.
  • Surface: The asteroid's surface is believed to be relatively bright and reflective.
  • Potentially Hazardous: Yes, classified as a PHA due to its size and orbit.
  • Effect on Earth:
    • If it were to impact Earth, it could cause widespread damage, affecting a large region and leading to serious climate consequences.
    • Such a large asteroid could potentially cause a mass extinction event depending on the impact location.

(66391) 1999 KW4

  • Size: The primary component is around 1.3 kilometers in diameter, with a smaller secondary moonlet.
  • Shape: The asteroid has an oblong, "spinning top" shape.
  • Orbit: It has a highly elliptical orbit and passes near Earth periodically.
  • Potentially Hazardous: Yes, classified as a PHA due to its size and proximity to Earth.
  • Effect on Earth:
    • If it impacted Earth, it would cause catastrophic regional damage due to its size.
    • Its binary nature could lead to unique collision dynamics.
